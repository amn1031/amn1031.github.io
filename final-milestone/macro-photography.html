MACRO PHOTOGRAPHY

What is Macro Photography?

Macro photography is a type of photography that captures small subjects, like bugs or flowers, close-up. This can be done in a studio or outdoors. Macro photography only occurs when you capture small subjects with a magnigication of life size or greater. Meaning, your subject is the same size as the camera sensor and fills the frame.

Magnification

Magnification is how large or small a subject appears on a camera sensor versus the subject's size in the real world. If the ratio is one-to-one, then the subject is known to be life size magnification. An example of this is if the subject is one inch in length and the camera sensor is one one inch wide, then the subject is considered to be life size.
